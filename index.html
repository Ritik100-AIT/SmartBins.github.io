How it Works

Smart bins are an intelligent waste management system. They have wireless ultrasonic fill-level sensors embedded inside which detect how full the bin is and then, through the IoT, this data is sent to a cloud-based monitoring and analytics platform. On the basis of this data, waste collection services can optimise their routes and frequency. Smart bins also use a solar-powered trash compactor which activates at a pre-set level, enabling them to hold a great deal more waste (up to 8 times) and avoid overflowing. Intelligent safety sensors are also installed which stop compaction if a hand is detected and, if fire is detected, will alert the monitoring station and automatically begin extinguishing the fire..

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Q1. What sensors are used in these dustbins to be able to call them ‘smart’?

    Ans. The ‘smart dustbins’ developed in this project make use of the ultrasonic sensor – to measure the dustbin fill-level and humidity sensors – to differentiate between wet and dry waste. The two sensors together allow the detection of level of waste in the dustbins and their type; solid or dry.

  • Q2. How would these dustbins help in waste management?

    Ans. User will get to know about real-time garbage level. Whenever the garbage level crosses threshold level, an alert will be generated for urgent collection of garbage. The data of wet and dry segregation level will help in evaluating the current garbage management plans and also to refine the plans for increasing the efficiency.

  • Q3. “Segregation has been a major concern in India and lack of systems that support segregation another.” Does the project answer this comment to an extent?

    Ans. The concept of smart dustbins is definitely one of the points to forge forth from in the direction of waste segregation. The government has also liked the concept and shown its support by allocating a significant fund to the initiative. Also, Smart waste management through the installation of smart waste bins is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 64.1% to touch US$5.42 billion in 2025.

  • Q4. How does the project aim to help the society and waste collectors?

    Ans. It has been found out that there is a potential of extracting at least 15 per cent of the daily waste generated in the country using smart waste management. The project could also be beneficial to atleast 50,000 ragpickers by providing them employment opportunities. E-waste segregation could also result in the improvement of the health conditions of waste collectors

  • Q5. Effects of the project on the economy?

    Ans. The informal recycling industry would make sure that less wate reaches landfills. This, in turn, would reduce the landfill management cost. More portion of solid waste management budget could be allotted for processing or resource recovery and disposal. Segregated waste could be converted into renewable energy and organic manure, hence, contributing towards the economic development or urban and rural areas.
